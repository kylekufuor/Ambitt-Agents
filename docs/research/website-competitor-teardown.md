# Website Competitor Teardown — Agent-as-Worker Marketing Sites

Author: Rex (Research) · Date: 2026-07-23 · For: ambitt.agency homepage design

**Scope:** Section-by-section teardown of the best use-case-led AI-agent / automation marketing sites, read live in July 2026, to inform ambitt.agency. We sell **named AI agents that do real work on the tools a business already uses and deliver via email/text** — so the lens throughout is: which patterns sell "an agent that does the job on your stack," and which don't fit a no-dashboard, deliver-by-inbox product.

**Sites read live (July 2026):** Lindy, Artisan (Ava), 11x (Alice/Julian), Sierra, Decagon, Gumloop, Bardeen, Cognition/Devin. Plus notes on Manus and Relay.app (shut down).

The single most important extraction — per the brief — is **how each site shows "what the agent does."** That column gets the most detail.

---

## 1. Lindy — `lindy.ai` — THE closest analog to us

Named agent + delegate-by-text + does admin on your existing tools. This is the site to study hardest.

- **Hero.** Headline: **"hey, I'm Lindy"**. Subhead: **"I take admin work off your plate."** Primary CTA: **"Try for free."** Primary visual: an **animated iMessage thread** — Lindy replying with completion checkmarks ("✓ Done, ✓ Sent, ✓ Booked, ✓ Moved") to prompts like "Clear my inbox · Prep for my 2pm · Reply to Mark." The hero *is* the delivery surface (a text thread), not a dashboard screenshot. This is a deliberate 2026 relaunch as a personal AI EA operated over iMessage/SMS ([Zapier review](https://zapier.com/blog/lindy-review/), [Lindy iMessage docs](https://docs.lindy.ai/features/imessage-sms)).
- **How they show what the agent does.** First-person **capability sections**, one job per full-width block, each with a real desktop screenshot: (1) "I organize your inbox, and draft your replies"; (2) "I schedule your meetings, and manage your calendar"; (3) "I brief you before every call, and follow up after"; (4) "I track your to-dos, and handle your follow-ups." Each section isolates *one* concrete task the agent owns end-to-end — verbs, first person, outcome-framed. No feature grid, no "workflow builder." The agent narrates its own job.
- **Works on your existing tools.** Repeated **logo wall** (Shopify, Adobe, NVIDIA, Airbnb, Retool, Autodesk, AppLovin, plus universities) as social proof, and one narrative line that names apps in-flow: *"With hundreds of integrations, Lindy can read your **Slack**, cross-reference your **calendar**, and draft a response in **Gmail**, all without you asking."* Naming specific apps inside a sentence about a task is far more convincing than a bare grid.
- **Visual language.** Modern sans-serif, near-monochrome (black on white) with a **teal accent** (note: our brand teal is `#00b3b3` — near-identical family), heavy whitespace, alternating left/right image blocks. The only motion is the hero text thread. Vibe: calm, human, premium, un-templated. The lowercase conversational headline signals "colleague," not "SaaS."

**Steal:** first-person job sections; text-thread-as-hero; apps named inside task sentences.

## 2. Artisan — `artisan.co` — named agent, aggressive persona

- **Hero.** Headline: **"Hire Ava — The autonomous AI BDR."** Subhead: **"She finds leads, sends personalized outreach, handles objections and books meetings at a fraction of the cost of a human BDR."** CTAs: **"Get started"** + **"Book a demo."** Visual: the **Ava character** (illustrated persona) over a glow-lit product graphic. Note the framing verb: **"Hire."**
- **How they show what the agent does.** A **4-step "she does X" numbered sequence**, each with a product screenshot: (1) "She finds and prioritizes high-intent leads" (250M+ contacts, 22+ data sources, intent signals); (2) "She launches personalized multi-channel sequences"; (3) "She tests and optimizes messaging automatically"; (4) "She handles replies and books meetings autonomously." Then three vertical use-case blocks (cold outbound / CRM re-engagement / account expansion). Third-person female pronoun throughout — the agent is a coworker with a name.
- **Works on your existing tools.** "Run Ava alongside your GTM stack" logo wall — **HubSpot, Salesforce, Google, ZoomInfo, BigQuery** — plus the reassurance line: *"Ava integrates with your CRM, calendar, and data tools in minutes. No migration, no rip-and-replace."* That last clause is worth stealing verbatim in spirit — it kills the #1 buyer objection.
- **Visual language.** Dark backgrounds, glow/gradient accents, character illustration, testimonial carousel. Premium-ish but leans "AI startup" theatrical.
- **CAUTION — the marketing lesson.** Artisan's growth engine was the **"Stop Hiring Humans"** billboard campaign (Times Square, SF, NYC): deliberately trollish anti-human slogans engineered for viral outrage. It generated reach *and* death threats and a dystopian-brand reputation; the CEO had to publish a walk-back post reframing it as "alongside" humans ([IBTimes](https://www.ibtimes.co.uk/controversial-stop-hiring-humans-campaign-1797096), [SF Standard](https://sfstandard.com/2025/04/07/the-real-person-behind-san-franciscos-hated-anti-human-ad-campaign/), [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/video/stop-hiring-humans-startup-artisan-hires-humans)). **For us this is the anti-pattern.** Our First-Truth / "makes the business better" positioning is the *opposite* of "replace your people." Borrow Artisan's clean "she does X → outcome" section mechanic; reject the replacement-fear brand entirely.

## 3. 11x — `11x.ai` — named "digital workers," credibility cautionary tale

- **Hero.** Headline: **"The AI Growth Company."** Subhead: **"Trusted by leading Sales, RevOps, and Marketing teams."** CTA: **"Get a Live Demo."** Visual: space/astronaut illustration (the "digital worker" metaphor). Two named agents: **Alice** (outbound SDR) and **Julian** (inbound phone agent), each with a status-chip product card ("Autopilot activated" / "Active").
- **How they show what the agent does.** Layered: named agent cards → a 6-tile benefits grid ("Always learning," "Deeply integrated," "24/7 Digital Workforce," etc.) → a **4-step interactive walkthrough** (Identify → Research → Personalize → Engage) with UI screenshots → testimonial carousel with hard metrics ("9.7% reply rate," "5x qualified meetings") → a **"Listen to Julian"** audio player (hear the agent actually work). The audio demo is a strong "proof it's real" device.
- **Works on your existing tools.** "Deeply integrated" section; names **Salesforce, HubSpot, Gmail, Google Calendar, LinkedIn, Slack, G2**. Framed as "works within your entire tech ecosystem."
- **Visual language.** Dark/charcoal, bold sans headlines, blue UI accents, generous whitespace, enterprise-minimalist.
- **CAUTION — the credibility lesson.** TechCrunch (Mar 2025) reported 11x **listed companies as customers that weren't** (ZoomInfo, Airtable both denied it; ZoomInfo: *"we are not a customer"*), inflated ARR by counting 3-month trials as annual, and ran **70–80% churn** ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2025/03/24/a16z-and-benchmark-backed-11x-has-been-claiming-customers-it-doesnt-have)). **Lesson for us:** the metric-heavy, logo-heavy "outcomes" wall only works if every number and logo is real and permissioned. Given our First-Truth principle, we should show *fewer, verifiable* proofs (a real client output, a dated result) over an impressive-but-hollow metrics grid.

## 4. Sierra — `sierra.ai` — premium enterprise "brand agent"

- **Hero.** Headline: **"Better outcomes. Built on Sierra."** Lead line: *"Introducing Horizon: Long-horizon agents that get more intelligent with every interaction."* CTA: **"Learn more"** (demo-led, no self-serve). Visual: Agent Studio dashboard screenshots.
- **How they show what the agent does.** Product-component scrolling breakdown rather than job stories: **Ghostwriter** (build from SOPs/transcripts in plain English), **Insights** (Explorer / Monitors / Experiments / Observability cards), **Horizon** (planning, context, outcome optimization). This is a *platform* narrative aimed at enterprise buyers — impressive but abstract; you don't viscerally see "the agent did my task."
- **Works on your existing tools.** A database-node diagram wired to **Google Cloud, Databricks, Snowflake, Redis, AWS** — infrastructure integrations, not end-user SaaS. Signals "enterprise-grade data plumbing," not "operates your Gmail."
- **Visual language.** Restrained, corporate-premium; navy/purple accents, security badges, lots of whitespace, minimal motion. The most "grown-up" site in the set — trades warmth for authority.
- **Takeaway:** great reference for *premium restraint* and trust cues; wrong reference for *use-case-first* — it's platform-first, and its integration story is infra, not "your tools."

## 5. Decagon — `decagon.ai` — outcomes + natural-language "AOPs"

- **Hero.** Headline: **"The AI concierge for every customer."** Subhead: **"Build, optimize, and scale AI agents that treat every customer like the only one."** CTA: **"Get a demo."** Visual: conversation screenshots with concrete resolved outcomes ("I've applied your membership perks," "Extending your rental for the weekend," "You're rebooked for a spa appointment"). Showing the *result sentence the agent sent* is a clean way to make "what it does" tangible.
- **How they show what the agent does.** Sequence: customer logo wall (Chime, Duolingo, ClassPass) → metric testimonial cards (70% resolution, 80% deflection, 95% cost reduction, $1M revenue) → **"Agent Operating Procedures (AOPs)"** — define workflows in *natural language* → a **Build / Optimize / Scale** accordion → an **"Omnichannel by design"** tabbed section (Voice / Chat / Email, each with a screenshot). The natural-language-workflow framing ("just describe it in plain English") maps almost exactly to how we scaffold agents via Atlas.
- **Works on your existing tools.** Weak spot: **logo walls only, no named integrations** on the homepage (just an "/integrations" link). Enterprise CX buyers infer it; a task-on-your-tools buyer wouldn't be convinced. Don't copy this omission.
- **Visual language.** Clean modern sans, white bg, blue CTAs, accordion/tab interactions, ROI-grid. Professional, results-driven, low-motion.
- **Steal:** result-sentence screenshots as hero proof; "describe the job in plain English" framing; channel tabs (ours = Email / Text).

## 6. Gumloop — `gumloop.com` — best "works on your tools" storytelling

- **Hero.** Headline: **"AI agents built by your team"** (also seen as "built for your team"). Subhead: **"Understanding a task should be the only prerequisite to automating it."** CTAs: **"Get Started"** + **"Talk to Sales."** Visual: **a live grid of integration logos** (Slack, GitHub, Gmail, Linear, Stripe, Snowflake, Airtable, Google Docs, HubSpot, Salesforce, Google Sheets, Exa, Dropbox, Asana, X) interwoven with **team-member headshots** (real named people, "builders"). The hero literally *is* the integration story — the tools are the star of the fold.
- **How they show what the agent does.** **Named agent cards by role**: Data Analysis Agent, Support Agent, CRM Agent, Meeting Prep Agent, Call Analysis Agent — each tagged by category (Data / Support / Sales) with builder avatars and a "Weekly Active Users" growth chart. Framed as **"Interact with agents like co-workers."** This role-card gallery is a clean, scannable "what can it do for me" map.
- **Works on your existing tools.** The strongest in the set: **three** logo walls, 30+ named apps, plus customer logos (Ramp, Instacart, Shopify, Gusto, Webflow, Samsara). Copy explicitly ties agents to "internal and external data." If our differentiator is "works on the tools you already use," Gumloop is the reference for *how much* to name and *how prominently*.
- **Visual language.** Modern SaaS, multi-color gradients, headshot-driven social proof, card layouts, whitespace. Friendly and human ("like co-workers"), though the gradients read a touch templated.
- **Steal:** agent-as-named-coworker role cards; heavy, specific app-naming; humanizing headshots.

## 7. Bardeen — `bardeen.ai` — tight use-case-per-section

- **Hero.** Headline: **"Find and reach leads no one else can."** Subhead: **"Source leads with our agentic web scraper, qualify them with AI and reach them with contact enrichment."** CTA: **"Get started."** Visual: **Bardeen Studio** product screenshot showing a real "qualified leads → Google Sheet" job. Outcome-in-hero, concrete.
- **How they show what the agent does.** Four **use-case sections in workflow order**, each a step of one real job with a screenshot: (1) "Extract data from any website" → (2) "Research leads with AI search" → (3) "Enrich with validated contact info" → (4) "Qualify leads with the power of AI." Reads as a single narrative of the agent doing an actual end-to-end task, not a feature list. Good model for "show one complete job, start to finish."
- **Works on your existing tools.** "Export your data" section naming **Google Sheets, Airtable, Notion, CSV** — plus "extract from *any* website" (the "works anywhere" claim, backed by the scraper). Note the pairing: a generic "any website" claim + specific named destinations.
- **Visual language.** Clean white, blue accents, subtle gradient glows, horizontal dividers between sections, Webflow-default sans, light on motion. Professional, product-forward.
- **Steal:** one-job-told-as-ordered-steps; "works on any website" + named export targets.

## 8. Cognition / Devin — `cognition.com` — premium restraint, "your tools"

- **Hero.** Headline: **"Cognition operates Devin, the first autonomous software engineer."** Subhead (mission-framed): *"We believe the purpose of technology is to expand human capacity — not by replacing meaningful work but by working alongside people..."* Note: this is the **explicit anti-"replace humans" stance** — the opposite pole from Artisan, and much closer to our First-Truth voice.
- **How they show what the agent does.** Narrative, not task cards: *"Devin plans, writes, tests, and ships production code on its own, working inside your codebase and the tools your team already uses."* One tight sentence with four action verbs + the "your tools" promise. Sparse, confident, low on screenshots — a deliberate "we don't need to oversell" posture. (Less use-case-rich than the others; included as the *premium-restraint + human-alongside* reference.)
- **Works on your existing tools.** Conveyed in-copy ("inside your codebase and the tools your team already uses") rather than a logo wall on the main page. Elegant but under-specified for a buyer who needs to see *their* app named.
- **Visual language.** Minimal, editorial, high-whitespace, restrained palette. Reads as the most "serious/expensive" brand alongside Sierra.
- **Steal:** the "works inside the tools your team already uses" line (almost our exact promise) and the mission-led, human-alongside framing.

## What to STEAL for ambitt.agency

1. **Named-agent, first-person job sections** (Lindy + Artisan). One block per job the agent owns, written in the agent's voice / "does X → outcome," each with a real screenshot or a real email/text it sent. This is the highest-converting "what it does" mechanic and it fits our named-agent product exactly.
2. **The delivery surface as the hero** (Lindy). Our hero visual should be an **email/text thread** — a client asking, the agent delivering finished work in the inbox — because "no dashboard, delivered to you" *is* our product. Don't lead with a dashboard screenshot; we don't have one for clients.
3. **Name the actual apps inside task sentences** (Lindy, Gumloop). "Reads your Slack, cross-references your calendar, drafts in Gmail" beats a silent logo grid. We route through Composio's 850+ apps — say the specific ones (Gmail, Slack, HubSpot, Salesforce, Google Calendar, QuickBooks, Notion) in the sentence describing the job.
4. **A prominent, specific integration wall** (Gumloop). Our core differentiator is "works on the tools you already use." Make it a hero-adjacent, densely-named wall + the reassurance line **"No new software. No migration. Your agent works in the tools you already have."**
5. **Result-sentence / real-output proof** (Decagon, Bardeen) — show the literal email/text the agent sent, not just metrics.
6. **Human-alongside, mission-led voice** (Devin, and our First-Truth principle) — "expands your team," never "replaces your people."

## What to AVOID

1. **Artisan's replace-humans fear brand** — it drew death threats and a forced walk-back; it directly contradicts our First-Truth principle. We are additive, never anti-human.
2. **11x-style unverified logo/metric walls** — the TechCrunch scandal (fake customers, 70–80% churn, inflated ARR) is the cautionary tale. Show fewer, real, dated, permissioned proofs.
3. **Decagon/Sierra "logo wall only, no named apps"** — too vague for a product whose whole pitch is *your* tools.
4. **Sierra/Devin platform-first restraint as the *whole* homepage** — beautiful, but not use-case-first; borrow their polish, not their abstraction.
5. **Manus blank-canvas hero** — hides the concrete outcome a buyer is paying for.
6. **Heavy gradients / character-mascot theatrics** (Artisan/Gumloop) — reads templated/AI-startup; we already committed to premium anti-AI-slop (portal `DESIGN.md`): elevation over borders, duotone icons, Lexend, cool-slate + teal `#00b3b3`.

## Recommended homepage skeleton (use-case-first)

1. **Hero.** Lowercase, human headline in the agent-team voice (Lindy pattern), e.g. *"Hire an agent that actually does the work."* Subhead names the model in one line: *"Named AI agents that work in the tools you already use and deliver finished work to your inbox — no dashboard, no logins."* Primary CTA: **"Meet your agent"** / **"See it work."** **Hero visual = an email/text thread**: client asks → agent delivers a finished deliverable (attached PDF/CSV), with a "done" beat. (Steals Lindy's delivery-as-hero + our real Resend/Twilio delivery model.)
2. **Integration wall + objection-killer** (Gumloop pattern), immediately under the fold. Dense, *named* app logos (Gmail, Slack, HubSpot, Salesforce, Google Calendar, QuickBooks, Notion, Airtable, Stripe…) + line: **"No new software. No migration. Your agent works in the tools you already have."**
3. **"What your agent does" — 3–4 first-person job sections** (Lindy + Bardeen). Each: one real job, told in the agent's voice, with a screenshot of a real email/text/attachment it produced, and the specific apps named in the sentence. Order them as complete jobs, not features.
4. **Meet the agents** (Gumloop role cards + Artisan "Hire" framing). A small gallery of named agents by role (EA, CRE sourcing, research analyst…) — each a coworker with a name, a face/avatar, and one line on the job they own.
5. **How it works — 3 steps.** Describe the job in plain English (Decagon AOP framing) → we scaffold & connect your tools → the agent works on its schedule and emails/texts you results. Reassure on control (pause/approve — we already ship autonomy modes + control-plane seatbelts).
6. **Proof, real and dated** (anti-11x). One or two genuine client outputs / results with dates; skip the vanity metric wall until numbers are real and permissioned.
7. **Trust + safety strip.** Human-in-the-loop, approval on big actions, data handling. (Our First-Truth + control-plane story is a differentiator most competitors bury.)
8. **CTA band.** Mission-led, human-alongside close (Devin voice): *"Your agent makes the business better — or it doesn't ship."* Single CTA to onboard.

**Guardrails:** keep the existing premium anti-AI-slop system (Lexend, cool-slate `#33475b`/`#f5f8fa`, teal `#00b3b3`, duotone icons, elevation-not-borders) from `client-portal/DESIGN.md`. Motion: one tasteful hero animation (the text/email thread resolving), nothing else — restraint reads premium (Sierra/Devin), gradients-everywhere reads templated (Gumloop/Artisan).
